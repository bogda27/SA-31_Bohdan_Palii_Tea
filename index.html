Tea - beverage for Gods

White tea

White tea is a type of tea that has a delicate flavor and is naturally low in caffeine. It is harvested at the start of the season, and is comprised of buds and new leaves.

Green tea

Made from the same leaf as black tea, but picked younger for a fresher, lighter flavor, our classic green tea blend is a delicate balance of grassy notes and natural earthiness that makes for a hydrating mid- morning pick-me-up or a delicious afternoon refresher.

Puer tea

pu-erh tea has a deep, rich flavor that many consider earthy or mushroomy. Good quality pu-erh often appeals to coffee drinkers and pairs well with rich desserts. The flavor and reputed health benefits of pu-erh tea also make it a great option for drinking as a digestif after a heavy meal; indeed, in China and Hong Kong, it is often consumed during and after heavy or greasy meals, like dim sum.

Black tea

Black teas tend to be bold and brisk, and they are often described as astringent. Black tea was the first type of tea to be introduced to Europe and the Middle East.

Oolong tea

Oolong tea is a traditional Chinese tea. It's made from the leaves of the Camellia sinensis plant, the same plant used to make green tea and black tea. The difference is in how the tea is processed. All tea leaves contain certain enzymes, which produce a chemical reaction called oxidation.

Yellow tea

Yellow tea sometime is also referred to as micro-fermented tea. It is a step up from green tea where during the making the tea is allowed to fermented a little before being completely dried
